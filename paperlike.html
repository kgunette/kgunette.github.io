An unlikely path to product through art

Hi Jan and the Paperlike team,

Your Head of Product role is the first I've seen that asks for two things at once: a product owner's judgment, which is where I've spent the last fifteen-plus years building digital products and founding a social impact tech company, and real physical-production experience, which is where I spent the decade before that, working in galleries and as an artist's assistant producing installations and objects for artists like Kimsooja and Tom Otterness.

While the materials are different, the shape of work is the same: understand the challenge or idea, create prototypes and test them, collaborate with others to build it, and then launch or exhibit it.

How I work

01

Craft and rigor

I care about how a thing is made and whether it actually works, which requires success metrics and controls from the start. I don't overpromise, and when I get something wrong, I own it, and fix it.

02

A point of view on how we build, and who we build for

The things we make should respect the people who use them, and many of them can surprise and delight as well. That belief shapes what I build and how.

03

Ownership from idea to launch

I enjoy the whole arc from strategy to execution, and it's what I mean by 0-to-1 as well. While a lot of people say they're good at it, my experience is that it's rarer in tech than it should be.

04

Commitment to communication

Collaboration runs on clear, regular communication, so that everyone knows what's happening and what's next. How I do it shifts with the person: an engineer, a partner, and a funder each need to know something different, but the commitment to surfacing relevant context to each doesn't change.

05

Knowing when good is good enough

I won't ship something weak, and I won't sit on something good waiting for perfect either.
