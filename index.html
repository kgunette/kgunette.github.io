I help organizations build things that don't exist yet. Usually that means a big idea, real urgency, and no playbook. My job is to figure out what to build, make a plan that holds up, and get it moving.

For 25 years across a lot of contexts: I founded and ran a national nonprofit from an idea to Congressional testimony and cowrote a policy brief with The Aspen Institute. I've been an early hire at startups that became major companies. I've worked on digital strategy for Samsung, Verizon, and Spotify as part of agency teams.

Three kinds of work I help organizations with

Launches

+

Building something from scratch

You secured funding for an idea, and now you need someone to shape it into a strategy and a product or program people actually use.

+

Running internal experiments

Your organization wants to test new ideas but your innovation team is lean. You need someone who can take experiments from concept to proof of concept.

Turnarounds

+

Fixing what isn't working

You're past the idea stage but it's not working the way you thought it would, and you need someone to diagnose the problem and help fix it.

+

Re-envisioning a program

You built something that worked for its original audience, but the landscape has shifted and you've hit a ceiling with your current approach. You need someone to rethink the strategy without blowing up what works.

Pivots

+

Exploring options at a crossroads

You'd like to explore options, both viable and risky. You need someone to help you scenario-plan, pressure-test each path, and create a clearer picture of what the way forward can actually look like.

+

Implementing AI thoughtfully

Your organization has an AI mandate but nobody's sure how to implement it thoughtfully. I get into the weeds of what you do and build a plan with your team that everyone can apply confidently.

How I'm thinking about AI

I've been thinking about AI implementation since 2023. I wrote organizational AI policy and prototyped AI tools at Leap Fund, and I've gone deeper since, building more complex prototypes and working through the real questions about how organizations can use it equitably and responsibly. We're in a real moment of flux, and I want to be in the room where organizations are making decisions about how to use it well.
